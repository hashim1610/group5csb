Disaster Crisis Identification from Twitter postsbased on Deep Learning Models

introduction


The word Disaster implies a sudden overwhelming and unforeseen event that influences a large number of people. Critical disasters around the world require quick assistance for lessening the consequences. Whenever a disaster occurs, the news can be disseminated widely through Social medias quickly. Given the vast amount of information on social media, finding relevant information on disaster in an effective manner may be problematic. Social media posts can be classified as follows for an efficient exploration.

  • Non-Relevant Posts : Posts for promoting awareness, providing advice, or expression of empathy about disasters.
  • Relevant Posts : Posts about disasters currently happening where victims may need immediate help and support.

problem statement


Social medias platforms are highly used during crisis. It spreads awareness, letting the emergency services to reach the location in no time for helping the victims. But the Data produced through micro-blogging platforms, like Twitter, contain huge volume of information, that may or may not be relevant to an immediate disaster. Hence, using social media as a tool, we identify the Relevant and Non-Relevant posts on Disasters using Deep Learning Models to organize relief efforts, find help, and potentially save lives.

Objective

  1. To develop an efficient Disaster crisis identification model by using socialmedia posts from twitter.
  2. To Construct dataset from social media with bi-class categorization.
  3. To perform data pre-processing using Word embeddings with GloVe.
  4. To adopt different deep learning models for training.
  5. To assure the robustness of the validation and to prevent overfitting.
